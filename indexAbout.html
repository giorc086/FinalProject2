About

Snow Matters is a project that visualizes historical weather data in order to understand when a skiing location has the best conditions. Snow Matters strives to become a resource for neutral weather reports that can be trusted and used to make informed decisions about where to go skiing. In this initial prototype the skiing conditions of only four locations are provided but the idea is to continue expanding to every skiing resort in the world.

Measurements
All measurments are using the imperial system. In a future development the option of choosing between imperial and metric will be offered.

Overview by Month - Radar Charts
The radar charts on the first page show the wetaher data by month. The months go from November to April which is the usual skiing season in the US.
Data source: Weather Underground

Overview by Conditions - Radar Charts
The radar charts on the first page can also be visualized by conditions. The four main conditions relevant to skiing are: snow, temeprature, visibility and wind.
Data source: Weather Underground

Average Ski Day Quality - Donut Charts
The donut charts below visualize the amount of "good", "ok", and "bad" ski days there are per month. The resuts were achieved by collecting historical weather data from 2010 to 2015. The conditions used to reach the skiability index per month are: temperature, visibility and wind.
Data source: Weather Underground

Average Snowfall - Bar Charts
The bar chart below represents the average snowfall per month in Vail, CO.
Data source: WeatherDB
